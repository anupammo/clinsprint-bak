Regulatory Affairs

What is regulatory Affairs?

Regulatory Affairs is the knowledge and application of strategic and operational enforcement of the legal frame work concerning pharmaceuticals and related products in a national and global perspective.

Regulatory Affairs is a profession within regulated industries, such as pharmaceuticals, medical devices etc.Regulatory affairs (medical affairs) professionals usually have responsibility

for the following general areas:

  • 1 Ensuring that their companies comply with all of the regulations and laws pertaining to their business.
  • 2 Working with federal, state, and local regulatory agencies and personnel on specific issues affecting their business.

i.e. working with such agencies as the Food and Drug Administrationor European Medicines Agency

Why regulate?

Drugs are not ordinary consumer products

  • 1 To ensure quality, safety and efficacy of drug products in order to assure the continued protection of Public Health.
  • 2 Professional advice needed
  • 2 “Hidden quality”

Pharmaceutical regulation – driven by disasters

Pharmaceutical regulation – driven by disasters

  • 1 Unfortunate events development of medicines regulation during 20th century
  • 2 1937 – Sulfonamide - diethylene glycol poisoning, 100 people died US Federal Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act and the birth of New Drug application
  • 2 1956 – thalidomide disaster, Neurosedyn. 10000 newborn with deformities.Major trigger for development of the modern controls on the sale and supply of pharmaceuticals

The modern era of drug regulation

There is a wealth of Regulations, Directives and Guidelines controlling the sale, supply and study of medicines.Agencies such as

  • 1 FDA (Food and Drug Administration) in US
  • 2 EMA (European Medicines Agency) in EU
  • 3 LV/MPA (National agencies (Läkemedelsverket) in SE set up to administer these rules and to approve medicines before they can be sold.

The role of regulatory affairs – application and approval phase

  • 1 Application Procedure
  • 2 Electronic submission
  • 3 Authority meetings/hearings
  • 4 Check progress of evaluation and anticipate questions (prepare answers to expected questions)
  • 2 Clarify raised questions, plan response and strategies with other departments
  • 2 Negotiate approval and Product Information with agencies
